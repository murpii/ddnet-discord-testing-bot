# Privacy Policy for the DDNet Map Testing Bot

**Last updated:** June 20, 2026 \
**Contact:** https://ddnet.org/staff/#contact

Here's a plain rundown of what the map testing bot does with your data on the DDNet
Discord server. It only covers the bot itself, whatever Discord does with your data
is covered by Discord's own Privacy Policy.

The short version: the bot has no database and isn't built to collect personal data.
It only uses what Discord already provides, and only to run the map testing process
(mappers submit maps, testers review them). We keep that data because we have a
genuine interest in running testing and keeping a record of how each map was decided,
and we never sell it or use it for ads.

## What it keeps

Pretty much everything it touches comes straight from Discord and is only there to
run testing:

- Your Discord username, display name, ID, avatar and roles, so it knows who did what.
- The messages in the testing channels, so it can follow the workflow.
- A simple tally of tester activity (ready and decline votes and the like) and a
  per-map changelog of what was decided, by whom, and when.

No emails, no phone numbers, nothing like that.

## The one thing worth knowing: test logs are public

When a map channel wraps up, the bot saves a "test log" of that channel and publishes
it on the DDNet website at https://ddnet.org/testlogs/, where anyone can read and
download it. That log includes the messages, the names and avatars of everyone who
took part, and the files shared in the channel (the `.map` file, screenshots, videos,
and so on).

So if you join a testing channel, please treat what you post there as part of a
public, permanent record of how the map was tested. That's the whole point of the
logs: anyone can look back and see how a map made it to release.

Aside from that, your data really only goes two places: Discord, where the bot lives,
and the DDNet website, where the logs are hosted. The bot also asks ddnet.org whether
a submitted map name is already taken, but that check sends nothing personal.

## How long it sticks around

There's no database, just files. Tester scores, changelogs and saved test logs stay
around for as long as they're useful as a record of testing, and the public logs on
the website are meant to be permanent.

## Your rights, and reaching us

Depending on where you live, you can ask to see, correct or delete the data the bot
holds about you, or raise a concern with your local data protection authority. Just
reach out at https://ddnet.org/staff/#contact. One honest heads-up: anything already
published in a public test log is part of a permanent record of how a map was tested,
so we may not be able to take it back out.

## Age

The DDNet server follows Discord's minimum age (13, or older where your local laws
require it), and the bot isn't meant for anyone younger.
